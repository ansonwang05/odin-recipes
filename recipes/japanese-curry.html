Japanese Curry

“Curry Rice” by Janne Moren is licensed under CC BY-NC-SA 2.0

Description

Japanese Curry or Curry Rice (Kare Raisu, カレーライス), is a common comfort food in Japan. It is also one of the most loved home cooked dishes in Japan and many Japanese people grew up eating curry rice as a kid.

Japanese curry is a curry with a stew-like consistency and commonly has onions, carrots, and potatoes. The sauce is usually thickened by a roux (mixture of fat, flour and spices). One can also add some protein or meats to the dish such as chicken, beef or pork.

This specific recipe will focus on making Japanese Chicken Curry but one can feel free to switch out the chicken for beef or pork or nothing at all.

Ingredients

  • 2 onions (large; 1.5 lb, 670 g)
  • 2 carrots (6.7 oz, 190 g)
  • 3 Yukon gold potatoes (15 oz, 432 g)
  • 1 tsp ginger (grated)
  • 2 cloves garlic
  • 1/2 apple (6 oz, 170 g)
  • 1 1/2 lb boneless, skinless chicken thighs (can be substituted)
  • freshly ground black pepper
  • 4 cups chicken stock/broth or water only
  • 1 package of Japanese curry roux (7-8 oz or 200-230 g)
  • 8 servings of cooked Japanese short-grain rice or any amount thats enough
